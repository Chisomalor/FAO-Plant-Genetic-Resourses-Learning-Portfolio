# Introduction to Plant Genetic Resources for Food and Agriculture

## Course Overview

This course introduced the importance of Plant Genetic Resources for Food and Agriculture (PGRFA) and their role in ensuring global food security, biodiversity conservation, and sustainable agriculture.

## Key Concepts Learned

- Plant genetic resources include seeds, landraces, wild relatives, breeding materials, and improved crop varieties.
- Genetic diversity is essential for developing crops that can resist pests, diseases, and climate change.
- Farmers, researchers, gene banks, and international organizations all contribute to conserving plant genetic resources.
- Conservation can be carried out **in situ** (in natural habitats or farmers' fields) and **ex situ** (gene banks, seed banks, field collections).
- International cooperation is necessary because countries depend on each other's genetic resources for crop improvement.
